# Messages for The Sweep - inspired by DCC book series

## Job briefing (title / intro screen)

> "SWEEP PROTOCOL INITIATED. The hero has vacated the premises. Structural integrity: nominal. Biological hazards: significant. You have been contracted to restore dungeon conditions to pre-incursion standards. Protective equipment has been provided. Results are expected. Good luck. You will need it."

---

## First pickup prompt

> "That's a mop. Industrial grade, pre-soaked. It won't make you feel heroic. It will, however, make certain things stop being on fire. Pick it up."

---

## Inventory full warning

> "You are carrying the maximum recommended load. This is a professional assessment, not a suggestion. Put something down."

---

## First enemy encounter

> "Ah. The corpse is moving. This is, unfortunately, within expected parameters. The hero's residual magic has a half-life problem. Deal with it. Then pick it up. Then dispose of it properly. In that order."

---

## Debris revert warning

> "That remains is becoming less inert by the second. You may want to address that."

---

## Debris reverts while on floor

> "You left it too long. It's angry now. This one's on you."

---

## Using debris as weapon against corrosive enemy

> "Unorthodox. Effective. The disposal report is going to be a nightmare to file."

---

## Disposal chute interaction

> "Disposal logged. The dungeon thanks you for your responsible waste management. It will not say so directly."

---

## Low HP warning

> "You are in suboptimal condition. The contract does not cover medical expenses."

---

## Job rating — A

> "Floor certified clean. Impressive. The dungeon hasn't seen this level of professional conduct since... well. Ever, actually."

---

## Job rating — C

> "Floor cleared. Technically. The word 'thorough' does not apply here. Moving on."

---

## Job rating — D

> "This is not what was agreed upon. The remaining hazards will be someone else's problem. You know this. You did it anyway."

---

## Supply closet

> "Resupply station. Everything here was left by previous contractors. Most of them finished the job. Take what you need."

---

## Final floor cleared

> "All floors cleared. Hazard index: nominal. The dungeon is ready to receive its next hero. They will make an enormous mess. You will not be surprised when they call again."

---

## Tutorial / first move

> "The dungeon operates on a strict turn-based protocol. You move. Things move. Nobody moves at the same time. This is not a suggestion. This is physics."

---

## First revert timer visible

> "Note the instability indicator. The remains are attempting to reconstitute. This is normal. This is also your problem."

---

## Picking up debris

> "Contained. You now have possession of a biohazard. Try not to think about it too hard."

---

## Dropping debris intentionally

> "You put it down. The clock restarted. You knew that. You did it anyway. Professional assessment pending."

---

## Splash flask AoE hits multiple enemies

> "Efficient. The quarterly report appreciates efficiency."

---

## Entering a new floor

> "Floor [N]. Previous contractor status: unknown. Proceed with standard caution. Or non-standard caution. Results will vary."

---

## Cursed enemy hit

> "The curse is attempting to transfer. The candle disagrees. The candle is winning, for now."

---

## Corrosive enemy damages a carried tool

> "Your equipment has been compromised. File a replacement request after you survive."

---

## All debris disposed, floor complete

> "Clean. The dungeon smells marginally less like catastrophe. This is progress."

---

## Death screen

> "Contract terminated. Cause: occupational. A replacement contractor will be sourced. The dungeon will wait."

---

## Potion use

> "Hero-grade restorative. Technically expired. Effective nonetheless. Don't read the label."

---

## New floor, all enemies visible from start

> "Initial assessment: manageable. Adjust expectations accordingly."

---

The voice should stay dry and institutional throughout — like an HR department that has fully accepted the existence of reanimated corpses and considers them a filing issue rather than a crisis. The humor comes from the gap between the bureaucratic tone and the absurdity of what it's describing. Perfect material for a voice actor who can play it completely straight.
